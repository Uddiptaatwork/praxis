**GW230529** (full ID: GW230529_181500), detected on 29 May 2023, was announced by the LVK collaboration in 2024. Based on the discovery paper ("Observation of Gravitational Waves from the Coalescence of a 2.5–4.5 M☉ Compact Object and a Neutron Star", Abbott et al. 2024):

| Component | Median | 90% Credible Interval |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|
| Primary (m₁) | ~3.6 M☉ | 2.5 – 4.5 M☉ |
| Secondary (m₂) | ~1.4 M☉ | ~1.2 – 2.0 M☉ |

The primary's mass falls squarely in the **"mass gap"** between the heaviest known neutron stars (~2.2 M☉) and the lightest known black holes (~5 M☉), making this event scientifically notable.
