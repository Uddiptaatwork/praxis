**GW230529** is a compact binary merger detected on 2023 May 29, announced by the LIGO–Virgo–KAGRA collaboration in 2024. It is notable because one component falls in the **lower mass gap** (roughly 3–5 M☉), making it ambiguous whether it is a neutron star or black hole.

The reported component masses (90% credible intervals) are:

| Component | Mass (M☉) |
|-----------|-----------|
| Primary (m₁) | 3.6⁺⁰·⁸₋₀.₅ |
| Secondary (m₂) | 1.4⁺⁰·⁶₋₀.₂ |

These are **source-frame masses** under the low-spin prior. The primary sits in the lower mass gap and could be either a very massive neutron star or a light black hole; the secondary is consistent with a neutron star.
