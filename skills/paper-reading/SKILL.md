# Reading a paper like a scientist

The goal is not a summary — it is a structured object the lab can reason over and attack. Read the actual source; never reconstruct a paper from memory of similar work.

## Extract, per paper

- **Claim** — the central result stated with its number *and* uncertainty. A claim that drops the error bar has been misread.
- **Method** — data used, model, inference technique, and the assumptions baked into each.
- **Stated assumptions / priors** — what the authors say they assume.
- **Unstated assumptions** — what also had to hold and went unmentioned. This is where the reader earns their keep.
- **Limitations** — what the authors flag, plus what they glide past.
- **Dependencies** — upstream results this leans on; if those are revised, does the claim survive?
- **Status** — consensus, contested, or single-group. Note who disagrees.

## Discipline

- Cite the specific source for every nontrivial claim. Paraphrase; quote rarely and briefly. Do not reproduce long passages.
- Separate what the paper *shows* from what it *argues* from what it *speculates*.
- If you cannot verify a detail from the source, say so. A gap named is better than a gap filled with a guess.
- Hand the team a brief compact enough to hold in working memory, with pointers back to the source for depth.
